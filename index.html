ORIGIN LEGEND

As long time ago, one of the Adivasi family lived in this forest were four brothers and one sister, whose name was Mogarkasa, Feshbaraja, Bhajimokasa, Jhadbaraja, and sister Pandharipen (Bhuridevi) used to live in this forest and they do digging work to run their family. Since there were no clothes in those days, they used to wrap the leaves of the trees around their bodies to cover them. The four brothers have strictly instructed their sister that she was not allowed to visit their workplace without playing the tapri or dabdi (a piece of a sheet made of tin) from a particular distance where they were doing their digging work in the forest. As always their sister Pandharipen (Bhuridevi) took Shidori (lunch) for them, One day she thought that she should meet her brothers without playing the tin sheet, She reached there and called their brothers for lunch. Suddenly seeing her there, the four brothers began to flee here and there as they were working naked. Feshbaraja hide in the forest of Bhandarbori, Bhajimokasaran to Ghogra hills and Zadbaraja fled to Seoni forest and Mogarkasa hide in this forest only. That's why Mogarkasa jungle named after one of the brothers Mogarkasa.

THE RESERVE

Mogarkasa Conservation Reserve – 30 km² of protected forest, situated near Salai, about 80 km from Nagpur. Part of the Ramdham–Ramtek–Nagardhan–Mogarkasa–Pench eco-circuit. In 2020 the Maharashtra Forest Department launched an eco-tourism project here.
